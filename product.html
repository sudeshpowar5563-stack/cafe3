Hot coffee

The history of hot coffee begins with its discovery in Ethiopia, where its stimulant properties were first noted, leading to its spread to Yemen and the Arabian Peninsula around the 15th century. Arabs were the first to roast and brew the beans with hot water, and coffeehouses became social centers in the 16th century. The beverage eventually reached Europe in the 17th century, with its cultivation expanding to colonies in India, Indonesia, and the Americas, eventually becoming a globally popular drink

Mirch-masala

The term "Mirch-masala-pizza" can refer to a dish offered by the Mirch Masala restaurant chain or a concept of Indian-spiced pizza. The restaurant chain, founded in 1992 by Rushad Ginwala, was inspired by Punjab's roadside 'dhaba' restaurants and has since expanded internationally, with its "Mirch Masala" brand offering a fusion of Indian flavors. Separately, other restaurants, such as Mirchi Cafe in Dublin, California, also offer dishes with similar names, like a "Mirch Masala Pizza," which features a blend of spices and halal meats.
